About Life Again Foundation

Born from one woman's journey through cancer, built for every person who needs to know they are not alone.

Who we are

Who We Are

Life Again Foundation is a charitable organisation working to reduce the social and economic impact of cancer in India. We exist to ensure that a cancer diagnosis is never faced in isolation — and that recovery is supported not just medically, but emotionally, financially, and socially.

Founded by acclaimed actor and cancer survivor Dr. Gautami Tadimalla, the Foundation channels her personal experience into a mission far larger than herself: building a connected, compassionate ecosystem of care that reaches the people who need it most, across every section of society.

Our work spans three deeply connected areas — health, education, and agriculture — because we believe that lasting change comes from strengthening the whole community, not treating a single problem in isolation.

Our founder

Dr. Gautami Tadimalla

For over three decades, Gautami Tadimalla has been one of India's most recognised and respected figures in cinema, appearing in more than a hundred films across Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam, Kannada, and Hindi. But it was a chapter far away from the screen that would define her most enduring legacy.

In 2004, Gautami was diagnosed with breast cancer. The experience changed everything. Moving through diagnosis, treatment, and recovery, she came face to face with a truth that statistics rarely capture — how lonely, frightening, and isolating cancer can be, and how much the presence of a supportive community can transform that journey.

She also recognised a harder reality: that the support she was able to access was out of reach for millions of Indians, simply because of where they lived or what they could afford. Cancer, she realised, was not only a medical crisis — it was a social and economic one.

Out of that understanding, Life Again Foundation was created. Gautami's belief is simple and unwavering: life does not end with a diagnosis — it begins again. In 2022, in recognition of her social activism, she was conferred an Honorary Doctorate in Social Welfare and Community Health by Asia Metropolitan University, Malaysia, and appointed Adjunct Professor. A TEDx speaker who has lectured at universities, corporates, and social organisations across India and abroad, she continues to use her voice, her recognition, and her lived experience to advocate for equitable cancer care, early detection, and dignity for patients and survivors across the country.

Our story

Our Story

Life Again Foundation began with a single conviction — that no one should have to face cancer alone — and grew into a movement.

Recognising the need for community, Gautami founded the Foundation with Ms Hyma Reddy to provide compassionate, functional support and to bring existing welfare policies within reach of disadvantaged communities. What started as the sharing of one survivor's experience has become a sustained effort to consolidate the patient-care ecosystem in India: spreading awareness, encouraging early detection, supporting patients through treatment and beyond, and extending help into education and rural livelihoods. Over the years, the Foundation has conducted hundreds of motivational camps, cancer awareness campaigns, and health and screening camps, while championing wellness, preventive care, and traditional approaches to health and healing.

Every initiative is guided by the same idea that founded us: that recovery is not just about surviving, but about reclaiming a full, hopeful, meaningful life.

What drives us

Mission, Vision & Values

Our Mission

To reduce the social and economic impact of cancer in India by making awareness, early detection, and compassionate support accessible to every section of society — and to extend that spirit of care into education and community welfare.

Our Vision

A society where no one faces cancer alone, where early detection reaches even the most underserved communities, and where every person — regardless of background — has the support, dignity, and hope to begin again.

Compassion

We treat every person we serve with empathy, dignity, and respect.

Equity

We bring care to those too often left out, across every section of society.

Transparency

We are committed to honesty and accountability in how every rupee is used.

Hope

We believe in the human capacity to rebuild, recover, and begin again.

What we do

What We Do

Health

Free cancer screening and detection camps, awareness drives, motivational camps, and preventive healthcare across India.

Education

Sponsoring underprivileged students and running supplementary education and vocational training centres.

Agriculture

Supporting rural and farming communities through sustainable livelihood and welfare initiatives.
