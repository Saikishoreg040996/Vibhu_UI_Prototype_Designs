EventSphere — Getting Into the App

A plain-English walkthrough of every sign-in and sign-up screen. No tech knowledge needed.

This document explains the authentication screens — the doors an organizer walks through before they reach the EventSphere dashboard. Think of it like the front desk of a building: before anyone gets inside, they either check in (sign up), show their pass (log in), or prove who they are (verify). Each screen below is one step. For every screen you'll see the story (what the person is trying to do), the actions (what they can click or type), and the states (what the screen looks like when things go right, go wrong, or are still loading).

The Big Picture — Two Front Doors

Door 1 — New organizer (first time here):

Sign Up → Verify email → Verify mobile (OTP) → lands in the onboarding setup wizard → Dashboard

Door 2 — Returning organizer (already has an account):

Log In → (if extra security is on) 2-step code → Dashboard

A few side-doors exist too: forgot password, a teammate accepting an invite, signing in with Google or Facebook, and the "you were logged out" screen. Each is explained below.

Door 2 · Returning Users

1 Log In

The screen a returning organizer sees first.

"I already have an EventSphere account. I just want to get back into my dashboard."

What they can do

  • Type their email or mobile number and their password.
  • Tap the little eye icon to show or hide the password while typing.
  • Tick "Remember me" so they don't have to log in every single time.
  • Press the big Log In button.
  • Or skip the password entirely and use Continue with Google / Continue with Facebook.
  • Tap "Forgot password?" if they can't remember it (goes to screen 6).
  • Tap "Create an account" if they're actually new (goes to screen 2).

What the screen looks like in different situations (states)

  • Ready normal — clean form, waiting for input.
  • Loading — after pressing Log In, a spinner shows while it checks the details.
  • Wrong password error — a red message says the email or password is incorrect.
  • Not verified yet warning — tells them to verify their email/phone first.
  • Locked out error — after too many wrong tries, it pauses login for safety.
  • Account suspended error — if the platform has paused their account, it says so and points to support.

Door 1 · New Users

2 Sign Up (Create Account)

The starting point for a brand-new organizer.

"I run trekking trips and I want to start selling tickets on EventSphere. Let me create my account."

What they can do

  • Type their full name.
  • Type their work email — the screen instantly tells them if that email is already used.
  • Pick their country code and type their mobile number.
  • Create a password — a little meter shows if it's weak or strong, with a checklist (at least 8 characters, one capital letter, one number, one special symbol).
  • Tick the box to accept the terms and privacy policy.
  • Press Create Account — or sign up instantly with Google / Facebook.
  • Tap "Already have an account? Log in" to go back to screen 1.

States

  • Ready normal — empty form.
  • Email already taken warning — suggests logging in instead.
  • Weak password — the checklist items stay grey until each rule is met, then turn green.
  • Loading — spinner while the account is being created.
  • Success done — moves them straight to email verification (screen 3).

3 Verify Your Email

Right after signing up.

"EventSphere just asked me to check my inbox and confirm my email is really mine."

What they can do

  • Read the "Check your inbox" message telling them an email was sent.
  • Go to their email and click the link inside it.
  • If nothing arrived, tap Resend email — this has a short countdown so it can't be spammed.
  • Tap "Wrong email? Change it" to correct a typo.

States

  • Waiting normal — "we sent you a link."
  • Resend on cooldown — the resend button is greyed out with a timer (e.g. "resend in 30s").
  • Verified done — a green success screen, then on to the next step.
  • Link expired / invalid error — the link is too old; offers a fresh one.

4 Verify Your Mobile (One-Time Code / OTP)

Confirms the phone number is real.

"I got a 6-digit code by text. Now I type it in to prove this is my number."

What they can do

  • Type the 6-digit code into six little boxes — it jumps to the next box automatically.
  • Paste the whole code at once and it fills all boxes.
  • Tap Resend code if it didn't arrive (with a countdown timer).
  • Tap "Change number" to fix a wrong phone number.

States

  • Ready normal — six empty boxes.
  • Checking — brief spinner after the last digit.
  • Wrong or expired code error — boxes turn red, asks them to try again.
  • Success done — verified, moves them into the setup wizard.

After screens 3 & 4: the new organizer is fully verified and handed over to the onboarding setup wizard (a separate set of screens where they build their profile, add their first event, set up payouts, etc.). The auth screens' job is done at this point.

Side-Door · Forgotten Passwords

5 Forgot Password

Reached from the "Forgot password?" link on the login screen.

"I can't remember my password. I need a way to reset it."

What they can do

  • Type the email or mobile number tied to their account.
  • Press Send reset link.
  • Tap "Back to login" to return.

States

  • Ready normal — one input box.
  • Sent done — "Check your email for a reset link," with a resend cooldown.
  • Not found — gentle message (kept vague on purpose, so it doesn't reveal who has an account).

6 Reset Password

Reached by clicking the reset link in the email/text.

"I clicked the reset link. Now I choose a brand-new password."

What they can do

  • Type a new password (same strength meter and checklist as sign-up).
  • Type it again in Confirm password to make sure they match.
  • Press Save new password.

States

  • Ready normal — two password boxes.
  • Passwords don't match error — red note until they match.
  • Link expired error — reset link too old; offers to send a new one.
  • Success done — "Password updated," then back to login.

Side-Door · Extra Security (2-Step)

7 Two-Step Verification (2FA)

Shown after login only if the organizer switched on extra security.

"I've turned on 2-step security. After my password, I also enter a code from my authenticator app."

What they can do

  • Type the authentication code from their app (or text).
  • Tick "Trust this device for 30 days" so they skip this step on their own computer.
  • Tap "Use a backup code instead" if they don't have their phone.
  • Press Verify.

States

  • Ready normal — code box.
  • Wrong code error — red message, try again.
  • Backup-code mode — swaps to a box for the one-time backup code.
  • Success done — straight to the dashboard.

Side-Door · Team Members Joining

8 Accept Team Invitation

Reached when a teammate clicks the invite link in their email.

"My boss added me to their EventSphere team. I clicked the invite and now I set up my own login."

What they can do

  • See which organization invited them and what role they've been given (Admin, Manager, Agent, or Support).
  • Set their name and a password — or join instantly with Google / Facebook.
  • Press Accept & join.

States

  • Valid invite normal — shows org name + role, ready to accept.
  • Loading — brief spinner while the account is set up.
  • Invite expired warning — the link is too old; asks them to request a new one.
  • Invite revoked error — the organization cancelled it; access is not granted.

Side-Door · Google / Facebook Sign-In

9 Google / Facebook Sign-In (Handover Screen)

A brief in-between screen while Google or Facebook confirms who they are.

"I chose 'Continue with Google.' There's a quick loading moment while it signs me in."

What happens

  • A short loading screen ("Signing you in…").
  • If their Google/Facebook email already has an EventSphere account, it may ask to link the two accounts so they become one.
  • On success, it drops them into the dashboard (or setup wizard if they're new).

States

  • Loading — "connecting to Google/Facebook."
  • Link needed warning — "You already have an account — link them?"
  • Sign-in failed error — something went wrong; offers to try again or use a password.

Side-Door · Getting Logged Out

10 Session Expired / Logged Out

Appears when someone has been idle too long or their login timed out.

"I left my screen for a while and came back — EventSphere logged me out for safety and asks me to sign in again."

What they can do

  • Read a friendly "Your session expired" message.
  • Press Log in again — and it remembers where they were trying to go, so they land back there.

States

  • Expired warning — polite notice, one clear button to sign back in.

One-Line Summary of Every Screen

  • 1. Log In — returning users get back in.
  • 2. Sign Up — new organizers create an account.
  • 3. Verify Email — confirm the email is real.
  • 4. Verify Mobile (OTP) — confirm the phone is real.
  • 5. Forgot Password — ask for a reset link.
  • 6. Reset Password — choose a new password.
  • 7. Two-Step Verification — extra security code at login.
  • 8. Accept Invite — a teammate joins an organization.
  • 9. Google/Facebook Sign-In — one-tap sign-in handover.
  • 10. Session Expired — friendly "please sign in again."
Every screen also works on all devices. Whether the organizer opens it on a phone, tablet, laptop, or desktop, the layout adjusts so nothing is cut off and every button stays easy to tap. On phones the screens stack into a single, thumb-friendly column; on big screens a friendly welcome panel sits beside the form.
